## Chapter 1: Executive Summary

**Payhome** is an AI-powered housing platform specializing in the **hiraya (single-story house)** market, originating from Kyushu, Japan.

**Business definition:**
A platform that supports the decision-making process of users considering hiraya homes, accumulates data from that process, and uses it to improve decision-making accuracy for both consumers and housing companies.

**Three-sided value proposition:**

| Stakeholder | Value Delivered |
|-------------|----------------|
| Consumers | Information organization, comparison, consultation, and action guidance throughout the hiraya decision-making journey. An AI concierge suggests next actions based on consideration phase and accompanies users to their optimal builder match |
| Housing companies (builders) | Not just lead referrals, but high-precision prospect contacts with intent data: interest tags, concerns, comparison targets, consideration phase, and engagement temperature |
| Platform (operator) | Build and operate a platform that evolves into an AI Housing Concierge and AI Sales Support SaaS, powered by accumulated behavioral, conversational, and intent data |

**Current business foundation:**
- 42,841 YouTube subscribers with 257 published hiraya room-tour videos
- 152,228 monthly views (84.8% new viewers)
- 12 builder partners across the Kyushu region
- 62-screen web platform (consumer site, B2B site, CMS, dashboards)

**Target position:**
Become the **decision-making infrastructure** for people considering hiraya homes in Kyushu — redesigning how information flows, comparisons are made, and recommendations are delivered in the hiraya market.

## Chapter 2: Market Background & Challenges

### 2.1 Growing Hiraya Market

Hiraya construction starts are increasing year over year, driven by:

- **Aging population**: Rising demand for barrier-free, compact housing
- **Dual-income households**: Growing interest in efficient floor plans with optimized housework flow
- **Lifestyle shifts**: Popularity of minimalist living and second-life housing
- **Kyushu characteristics**: Relatively affordable land prices make single-story construction feasible

### 2.2 Consumer Challenges

| Decision Stage | User Pain Point |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Research | Few trusted information sources specialized in hiraya. General housing portals make hiraya-specific comparison difficult |
| Builder selection | Hard to identify which builders specialize in hiraya. No time to visit showrooms |
| Comparison | Unclear comparison criteria for floor plans, performance, design, and pricing |
| Consultation | Want expert advice without sales pressure. Want to proceed at own pace |
| Decision-making | Fear of making a wrong choice on a once-in-a-lifetime purchase. Cannot prioritize what matters |

### 2.3 Builder Challenges

| Challenge | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Rising acquisition costs | Lead cost on portals like SUUMO rising to JPY 8,000-15,000 per lead |
| Low lead quality | Many "just browsing" requests. Builders cannot gauge seriousness or consideration phase |
| Digital gap | Lack of SNS, video, and web marketing know-how and resources |
| Inefficient meeting prep | Cannot know customer interests, concerns, or comparison targets before the first meeting |

### 2.4 Limitations of Existing Services

Existing housing portals simply "list properties and send inquiries." They do not support the user's consideration process, and builders receive no context about why someone reached out.

Payhome evolves beyond this **lead-referral model** into a **decision-support model**.

## Chapter 3: Value Proposition

### 3.1 Value to Consumers

**"Consider hiraya homes at your own pace, without the pressure."**

| Value | Features & Experience |
|-------|----------------------|
| Information organization | Hiraya-focused videos, articles, and case studies delivered systematically |
| Comparison support | Favorites, comparison lists, and browsing history to visualize consideration status |
| Consultation support | AI concierge available 24/7 with expert knowledge, no sales pressure |
| Action guidance | Phase-appropriate next actions: open house reservations, catalog requests, consultations |
| Decision support | Builder recommendations with reasoning, matched to user interests, budget, and family structure |

### 3.2 Value to Housing Companies

**"Improve the quality of leads, not just the quantity."**

| Value | Features & Experience |
|-------|----------------------|
| High-precision leads | Intent data attached: interest tags, concerns, comparison targets, consideration phase, engagement temperature |
| Pre-meeting intent summary | AI auto-generates summaries from chat logs and behavioral history |
| Proposal support | AI suggests "this customer should be pitched on X" before the meeting (future) |
| Customer acquisition | Room-tour video production, SNS management, web development |
| Sales improvement data | Conversion pattern analysis: what content closers viewed, how long they considered (future) |

### 3.3 Value Creation Structure

```
User considers a hiraya home
  ↓ Watches videos, reads articles, chats with AI, compares, saves favorites
Behavioral data + conversational data accumulates
  ↓
AI estimates intent (interests, concerns, phase, temperature)
  ↓
For users: Next-action suggestions, personalized recommendations
For builders: Intent-enriched leads, pre-meeting summaries
  ↓
Decision-making accuracy improves for both sides
  ↓
Close rate improves → Builder willingness to pay increases → Platform revenue grows
```

The core insight: **the user's consideration behavior itself becomes a data asset that continuously increases service value.**

## Chapter 5: Competitive Advantages

### 5.1 Five Competitive Advantages

| # | Advantage | Description | Defensibility |
|---|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | Hiraya-specialized first-party content | 257 hiraya room-tour videos. First-party information from actual filming and interviews — qualitatively different from secondary online content | High: Requires builder relationships and site access. Years for competitors to match volume |
| 2 | Contextual understanding of hiraya seekers | Knowledge of what 42,841 subscribers react to, watch, and comment on. Tacit understanding of hiraya seekers' interests, concerns, and comparison behaviors | High: Accumulated over years of YouTube operation. Cannot be replicated through data collection alone |
| 3 | Kyushu builder network | Trust relationships with 12 partner builders. Understanding of each firm's strengths, weaknesses, and customer profiles through direct interviews and filming | Medium: Takes time but can be replicated through dedicated sales effort |
| 4 | First-mover advantage in data accumulation | A platform that structurally captures browsing, consultation, reservation, chat, and comparison data. The earlier it runs, the larger the data gap with competitors | High: Data volume is proportional to time. Latecomers cannot catch up |
| 5 | Pathway to AI recommendations & proposal support | Accumulated data enables improved recommendation accuracy for users and intent summaries/proposal support for builders. An evolution path unavailable to simple media or portals | High: AI cannot function without the data foundation. Requires foundation + data + domain knowledge |

### 5.2 Competitive Differentiation

**Critical market development (January 2026):** Japan's only hiraya-specialized portal, "e-Hiraya net" (operated by Lib Work, launched in 2020), was permanently closed on January 31, 2026. **There is now zero hiraya-specialized platforms in Japan. Payhome will be the first and only hiraya-focused AI platform in the country.**

The closure of e-Hiraya net validates that a traditional listing-only portal approach cannot sustain value in the hiraya market. Payhome's differentiated approach — YouTube first-party content × AI × data accumulation — is the correct strategy, as proven by the failure of the listing-only model.

| Competitor Type | Examples | How Payhome Differs |
|----------------|----------|---------------------|
| General housing portals | SUUMO / HOME'S | Not hiraya-specialized. No video content. No decision-process support. Lead-referral model |
| Hiraya-specialized portal (closed) | e-Hiraya net (Lib Work) *Closed 2026/1/31* | Listing-only model that failed to sustain. Lacked YouTube, AI, and data accumulation infrastructure. Demonstrates the limitations of the listing approach |
| Housing YouTubers | Individual channels | Video only. No integrated Web/CRM/AI platform. No data accumulation infrastructure |
| Housing consultants | In-person services | Cannot scale. No digital data trails. Person-dependent |
| AI housing services | Generic AI chat | No hiraya first-party content. Shallow domain knowledge. No user behavioral data |

**Payhome's fundamental differentiation:** Hiraya first-party content × User behavioral data × AI utilization. No existing player holds all three. With the closure of e-Hiraya net, Payhome is Japan's only hiraya-specialized platform — a powerful message for builders and investors alike.

## Chapter 8: Data Strategy / AI Strategy

### 8.1 Data Strategy Principle

**"Turn every user touchpoint into AI training data."**

Payhome's business value originates from data naturally generated as users consider hiraya homes. Watching videos, reading articles, chatting with AI, adding favorites, comparing builders — every action becomes data that improves recommendation accuracy, raises lead quality, and increases platform value.

### 8.2 Data Types and Usage

| Data Type | Collection Method | Usage |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| Browsing history | page_view / video_view events | Interest estimation, content recommendations |
| Favorites | Explicit user save action | Identify properties/builders under consideration, recommendation material |
| Comparison history | Addition to comparison list | Estimate comparison criteria and decision factors |
| Chat logs | AI concierge conversations | Intent extraction, concern identification, intent estimation, summary generation |
| Form submissions | Consultation / catalog / reservation forms | Lead creation, inbound context recording |
| User profile | Progressive profiling, self-reported | Personalization, segment analysis |
| Engagement | Time on page, scroll depth, clicks | Content quality evaluation, interest level estimation |

### 8.3 AI Evolution Stages

```
Phase 1: Accumulate data
  - Record behavioral events
  - Persist chat logs
  - Unify user identifiers

Phase 2: Start using data
  - Simple recommendations from browsing trends
  - Intent extraction and tagging from chat logs
  - Behavioral context attached to leads
  - Consideration phase estimation

Phase 3: AI proposes
  - Per-user next best action estimation
  - Per-builder proposal optimization
  - Pre-meeting summary auto-generation
  - Conversion prediction model
```

### 8.4 Data Accumulation Flywheel

```
More users → More behavioral data → Better recommendation accuracy
  ↑                                          ↓
  └── Higher lead quality ← Higher builder satisfaction ← Higher close rate
```

Once this flywheel starts spinning, data volume and recommendation accuracy reinforce each other, creating an ever-widening data gap with later entrants.

### 8.5 Privacy and Data Ethics

- Behavioral data collection is based on explicit user notification and consent
- Personally identifiable data and behavioral data are stored separately
- Data provided to builders is aggregated and anonymized insights, not raw personal data
- Users can request to view or delete their own data

  ↓ [page_view / video_view events recorded]
